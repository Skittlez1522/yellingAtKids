How is weight affected by gravity?

Weight is affected by gravity because it is a direct consequence of gravity. The strength of the gravitational pull of an object makes you weight more or less. For example, you weigh more on Earth than you do on the moon because the moon has a weaker gravitaional pull. This is the case with everything in the universe as explained by Isaac Newton.
